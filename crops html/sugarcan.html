SUGARCANE CULTIVATION METHOD

SUGARCANE CULTIVATION METHOD

1.Land Preparation

• Field Selection: Choose well-drained, fertile soil, ideally loamy or sandy loam. Alluvial soil is great for sugarcane. • Ploughing: Plough the field 2-3 times to break up the soil and create a fine seedbed. Ensure proper field leveling for uniform irrigation. • Fertilization: Incorporate organic compost or farmyard manure (FYM) into the soil before planting. • Soil Testing: Conduct a soil test and apply fertilizers accordingly (NPK: Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium).

2. Seed Selection

• Varieties: Choose disease-resistant sugarcane varieties suited to the local climate. Common varieties include Co 86032, Co 94012, Co 62175, etc. • Seed Rate: Use 1,500 to 2,000 kg of setts (sugarcane stalks) per hectare for planting.

3. Sowing (Planting)

• Planting Time: The ideal time for planting sugarcane is during the monsoon (June to September) for optimal growth. • Method of Planting: Plant setts (sugarcane cuttings) horizontally or slanting on the prepared soil. Each sett should be 20-25 cm long, and rows should be spaced 75 cm apart. • Depth: Plant the setts at a depth of 5-7 cm to ensure good root growth.

4. Irrigation

• Water Requirement: Sugarcane is a water-intensive crop. It requires regular irrigation, especially during the early stages of growth and dry spells. • Irrigation Methods: Use drip irrigation or furrow irrigation for efficient water management. Ensure that the crop gets sufficient water during flowering and tillering stages.

5. Weeding and Pest Control

• Weeding: Control weeds manually or with herbicides, especially in the early stages of growth. • Pest and Disease Control: Monitor for pests like sugarcane borers and aphids. Diseases like red rot and smut can affect yield, so use fungicides or resistant varieties. • Pesticide/Herbicide: Apply as necessary, following the recommended guidelines.

6.Fertilization

• Fertilizer Application: Apply NPK fertilizers, with higher nitrogen levels during early growth. Split applications of fertilizers can be used to meet the plant’s growth demands. • Top Dressing: Apply additional nitrogen during the tillering stage, and a final dose of potassium and phosphorus during the ripening phase.

7. Harvesting

• Time to Harvest: Sugarcane takes about 12-18 months to mature, depending on the variety and climate. • Signs of Maturity: The plant’s leaves start drying, and the cane turns thick and firm with high sucrose content. • Harvesting Method: Harvest the canes by cutting them at ground level. A manual or mechanical harvester can be used, with the latter being more efficient for large-scale operations.

8. Post-Harvest Handling

• Cleaning: After harvesting, sugarcane should be cleaned by removing the leaves and roots. • Storage: The harvested cane can be stored for a short time before processing. Cane should be processed as soon as possible to avoid loss of sugar content. • Crushing: Cane can be crushed for sugar extraction or processed into various value-added products.

9.Expected Yield and Income

• Expected Yield: • Average yield: 60-80 tons per hectare. • High-yield varieties can produce 100 tons or more per hectare under optimal conditions. • Market Price: The price of sugarcane can vary, but it typically ranges between ₹3,000 to ₹5,000 per ton, depending on local market conditions.

10. Income Calculation:

• For 60 tons at ₹4,000 per ton: ₹2,40,000 • For 80 tons at ₹4,000 per ton: ₹3,20,000 • For 100 tons at ₹4,000 per ton: ₹4,00,000 Thus, the expected income will range between ₹2,40,000 and ₹4,00,000 per hectare, depending on yield and market prices.

11.Value-Added Products from Sugarcane

• Sugar Production • Molasses • .Jaggery (Gur) • Sugarcane Juice • Ethanol Production • Bagasse for Bioenergy • Biochar • Cane Syrup
