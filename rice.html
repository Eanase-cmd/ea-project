RICE CULTIVATION METHOD

Rice is one of the most important crops grown, particularly in alluvial soils. Here's a concise overview of the cultivation method, expected yield, and income, along with value-added products.

1.Land Preparation

• Field Selection: Choose flat, well-drained, and fertile fields. Alluvial soils are ideal for rice due to their fertility. • Ploughing: Plough the field 2-3 times to break the soil and create a fine seedbed. Levelling is essential to ensure uniform water distribution. • Flooding: Flood the field with 2-3 inches of water to soften the soil and control weeds. • Fertilization: Apply balanced fertilizers (NPK) based on soil tests to ensure healthy crop growth.

2. Seed Selection

• Choose certified, high-quality rice seeds that are suited to your region (Kharif or Rabi varieties). • Seed Rate: About 25-30 kg per hectare for direct seeding or 15-20 kg for transplanting. .

3. Sowing

• Direct Seeding: Seeds are sown directly into the waterlogged field (broadcasting or drilling method). • Transplanting: Raise seedlings in a nursery, then transplant them to the field when they are 20-25 days old. Maintain 20-25 cm spacing between plants and rows.

4. Irrigation

• Maintain a standing water depth of 2-3 inches throughout the crop's growth. Ensure proper drainage after flowering to avoid waterlogging during maturation.

5.Weeding and Pest Control

• Weeding: Manually remove weeds or apply herbicides in the early stages of growth. • Pest and Disease Control: Monitor for pests like rice weevil, leafhopper, and stem borer. Use pesticides or natural methods like neem oil if needed.

6. Fertilization

• Basal Fertilization: Apply a balanced fertilizer (NPK) at planting time. • Top Dressing: Apply nitrogenous fertilizers like urea at 30 and 60 days after sowing for optimal growth.

7. Harvesting

• Time: Rice is typically harvested 4-5 months after sowing when the grains turn golden yellow and the plant starts drying. • Harvesting Method: Cut the plants manually or using a combine harvester. Avoid harvesting too early to ensure full grain maturity.

8. Post-Harvest Handling

• Drying: Sun-dry the harvested rice to reduce moisture content to 12-14% to prevent mold growth during storage. • Storage: Store the dried rice in cool, dry, and well-ventilated conditions to preserve its quality.

9.Expected Yield and Income

• Expected Yield: • Kharif Season: 3,000 - 4,000 kg per hectare. • Rabi Season: 4,500 - 5,000 kg per hectare (with good irrigation). • Market Price: • Average price: ₹25-35 per kg (prices may fluctuate based on location, quality, and market conditions).

10.Income Calculation:

• For 3,000 kg at ₹30/kg: ₹90,000 • For 5,000 kg at ₹30/kg: ₹1,50,000 Total Income: Expected income will range between ₹90,000 to ₹1,50,000 per hectare, depending on yield and market prices.

10.Value-Added Products from Rice

• Rice Flour • Rice Bran Oil • Rice Husk (Biofuel) • Rice-based Snacks • Rice Noodles and Vermicelli • Rice Bran Cake (Animal Feed)
